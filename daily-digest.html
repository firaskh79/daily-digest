Outside the Frame

Sudan — a story that has fallen off the map

Sudan is now officially the world's hungriest country, and it has almost entirely disappeared from Western media. The IPC confirmed in May that famine conditions have spread to four areas of Darfur, with nearly 20 million Sudanese — roughly two in five of the population — experiencing crisis-level food insecurity or worse. Some 375,000 people face imminent starvation. The 2026 humanitarian response plan requires $2.9 billion; it has received approximately 5.5% of that.

Three years into a war that has killed hundreds of thousands, displaced 14 million, and collapsed the health system entirely, Sudan is living through a famine in near-total media silence — crowded out by Iran, Israel, Romania, and Ebola. The invisibility is structural: African humanitarian crises reliably lose column inches when European-adjacent crises dominate the cycle. Opening a new thread on this.
