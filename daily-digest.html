Changed Ebola — DRC / Uganda

As of 28 May: 125 confirmed cases, 906 suspected, 223 suspected deaths — slightly revised from the 1,010/231 reported on 26 May, reflecting reclassification. The WHO declared this a Public Health Emergency of International Concern on 17 May. An American healthcare worker who contracted Bundibugyo Ebola in the DRC has been transported to Germany for treatment and is in stable condition — the first export of the outbreak to a high-income country. Uganda has 9 confirmed cases, all linked to DRC travel. No licensed vaccine exists for the Bundibugyo strain. The PHEIC reflects institutional concern that containment is not certain even though spread beyond DRC and Uganda has not yet been confirmed.

Outside the Frame

Outside the Frame

Today's Times of Israel report on the UAE's covert military role in the Iran war deserves more attention than it is getting in Western coverage. The UAE — presented throughout the conflict as a neutral diplomatic hub that happened to be absorbing Iranian missiles — was in fact launching retaliatory strikes of its own, using French Mirage jets and Chinese Wing Loong II drones to hit targets on Iranian soil, including an oil refinery on Lavan Island in the Persian Gulf, coordinating directly with Israel both before and after the April ceasefire. Iran had fired 550 ballistic missiles and 2,200 drones at the UAE, making it the most-targeted country in the entire war by volume. The UAE struck back. It has still not publicly acknowledged any of this.

The significance is structural. The Abraham Accords were described as normalisation agreements — trade, tourism, diplomatic relations. What the UAE did during this war is something different: it operated as a military co-belligerent, coordinating targeting with Israel, using assets from two separate great-power supply chains simultaneously (French and Chinese). Anyone modelling the Iran-deal negotiations as a bilateral US–Iran affair should factor in that the regional alignment is deeper and more operationally tested than any public framing suggests. The Gulf is not a bystander that got caught in the crossfire. It is a participant that chose discretion.
