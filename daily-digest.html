Today's Signal

A conditional ceasefire in Lebanon — and Hezbollah already rejecting it

After two days of Washington talks, Israel and Lebanon agreed late June 3 to a framework creating "pilot security zones" in southern Lebanon under exclusive Lebanese Armed Forces control, with IDF withdrawal from those zones in exchange. Next talks are scheduled for the week of June 22. On paper this is the most significant diplomatic movement in weeks: it threads the needle between Israel's refusal to include Lebanon in the Iran ceasefire and Iran's insistence that it must be. Brent dropped to $96.97 today, pricing in reduced risk. Trump reiterated the US-Iran ceasefire is still "intact" despite overnight exchanges near Kuwait.

But Hezbollah's senior political official Mahmoud Qomati said the group "will not accept a partial ceasefire." Fighting continued on June 4 — at least nine killed in southern Lebanon. The architecture exists; it just needs a party that claims not to want it to stop shooting. The June 22 talks are the real test, not today's announcement.
