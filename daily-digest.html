Outside the Frame

Operation Southern Spear: 200+ killed in US drug-boat strikes — barely covered in UK

Since September 2025, the US military has killed more than 200 people in airstrikes on vessels in the Caribbean and Latin American Pacific waters that the Trump administration has alleged — without producing public evidence — were carrying drugs for Tren de Aragua (Venezuela) or Colombia's ELN guerrillas. The operation is called Southern Spear. The first airstrike, announced by Trump on September 2, killed all 11 people aboard a Venezuelan-flagged boat. In one of the most troubling documented incidents, the military killed survivors of the initial strike with a deliberate follow-up attack.

Human rights organisations, Colombia, Venezuela, and international legal experts have said the strikes are illegal under US and international law. Democratic lawmakers have called the killings extrajudicial murder. The Trump administration says it is legal and necessary. This story is receiving almost no coverage in UK media.

It matters to Firas's map for a direct reason: the Colombia election runoff is June 21. De la Espriella, the Trump-style candidate leading with 43.7%, is likely to accommodate the US posture in Latin American waters. Iván Cepeda (40.9%), continuity with Petro, would challenge it. The outcome of that vote will determine whether Latin America acquiesces or resists — and whether the 200 dead becomes the floor or the ceiling of this operation.
