Iran / Hormuz — Deal "Largely Negotiated"

President Trump said on Saturday that a peace agreement with Iran that would reopen the Strait of Hormuz is "largely negotiated" and will be announced shortly. The deal as reported would involve a 60-day truce with the Strait open, followed by broader talks on Iran's nuclear programme within that window. Iran's foreign ministry confirmed a memorandum of understanding is the intended first phase.

The core sticking point remains Trump's demand that Iran surrender its enriched uranium stockpile and permanently give up nuclear weapons capacity — something Tehran has been unwilling to accept as a precondition. A fragile ceasefire has been in place since April 8, punctuated by skirmishes as both sides continue jockeying for position. Oil markets moved sharply: Brent hit $103–105, up from $87 earlier in the month, and was last reported at +1.2% on the day.

Watch for: formal MOU signing; Iran's public position on the uranium question; whether Brent comes off sharply on announcement.

Cannes 2026 — Mungiu's Fjord Wins the Palme d'Or

Romanian director Cristian Mungiu won his second Palme d'Or at the 79th Cannes Film Festival on Saturday evening for Fjord, a culture-war drama starring Renate Reinsve and Sebastian Stan. It is Neon's seventh consecutive Palme — a run that speaks to the distributor's total grip on the prestige arthouse circuit. Mungiu's first Palme was for 4 Months, 3 Weeks and 2 Days in 2007. The Grand Prix went to Andrey Zvyagintsev's Minotaur; Best Director was shared between the Spanish team behind The Black Ball and Pawel Pawlikowski for Fatherland. Barbra Streisand received an honorary Palme via video message.

In acceptance, Mungiu was careful and pointed: "Today, the society is split, it's divided, it's radicalized. This film is a pledge against any kind of fundamentalism. It's a pledge for tolerance and inclusion and empathy." No direct reference to any specific conflict — but the timing, against the backdrop of Lebanon and Iran, made the resonance hard to miss in the room.

Watch for: Mungiu's press circuit and whether Fjord gets drawn into direct political commentary. Festival closes tonight.

White House Scraps AI Executive Order Hours Before Signing

The White House abruptly cancelled a planned AI executive order on May 21, hours before the scheduled signing ceremony. The order would have established a voluntary 90-day pre-launch review framework for frontier AI models, with NSA involvement in classified testing of the most powerful systems. No official explanation was given for the pullback. The reversal comes just as Anthropic reported its first quarterly operating profit and is closing in on a $900 billion valuation ahead of a potential IPO — a sign of how quickly the political economy of AI is shifting beneath any attempt to regulate it.
