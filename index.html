Mearsheimer: "We Should Be Deeply Worried" — Nuclear Escalation Risk in Ukraine and Iran

On May 18, John Mearsheimer appeared on Glenn Diesen's podcast to discuss escalation in both the Ukraine and Iran wars. His argument: in Ukraine, Europe is ramping up drone attacks on Moscow and threatening bigger ones; Russia has made clear the situation is "untenable" and that escalation — including possible attacks on NATO states — is being actively considered. The problem is structural: no bargaining space between the sides, no off-ramp. In Iran, the same structural logic applies: no bargaining space, and a Trump who oscillates between threatening to bomb and claiming a deal exists when no evidence of one does. His Substack posts from May 18 — "Putin's Incentive to Escalate" and "Will He or Won't He?" — are the most widely circulated analytical pieces on Ukraine this week. His conclusion: "The chances for truly dangerous escalation are real in both wars." This is not a moral argument; it is a deterrence logic argument. The incentive structures point toward escalation regardless of who is morally right.

Synthesis

Daily Theme

Culture Narrows While the World Widens

The overarching pattern today is a stark bifurcation: the macro world is expanding in danger — Hormuz, Lebanon escalation, Ukraine nuclear risk, fiscal stress, UK political fragmentation — while the cultural world, in its chart and social-media expression, is contracting toward the domestic, intimate, and personal. Olivia Dean's dominance of the UK chart is not incidental. Five songs in the top twelve, all foregrounding closeness and emotional directness, in a week of spiking bond yields and an active Lebanon war, is a form of cultural self-protection.
